About Ibaraki

Ibaraki Prefecture consistently supplies the largest volume of high-quality produce to the Tokyo Metropolitan Central Wholesale Market, where Japan's finest ingredients converge. Benefiting from its location within a 100-kilometer radius of Tokyo and excellent transportation links, Ibaraki boasts abundant water resources and a climate blessed with distinct seasons. This has enabled it to achieve the highest volume of produce handled at the Tokyo Metropolitan Central Wholesale Market in Japan.

Ibaraki Prefecture, home to Japan's largest market, boasts an abundance of high-quality, delicious vegetables and fruits. It features unique local brand ingredients and varieties cultivated exclusively within the prefecture, many of which are recognized by top chefs at Michelin-guide-listed restaurants. Among these, popular items include the Ibaraki King melon, an original Ibaraki melon with a perfect balance of meltingly sweet flavor and refreshing aroma; the Iinuma chestnut, considered the pinnacle among Japanese chestnuts; sweet potato chips, popular as a natural sweet treat among health-conscious consumers; and the Keisui pear, an original Japanese pear variety from Ibaraki.

One of Ibaraki Prefecture's defining features is its “diversity,” where the northern and southern limits of various species intersect within Japan. With a 190-kilometer coastline, Ibaraki's seas are one of Japan's premier fishing grounds where the warm Kuroshio Current from the south meets the cold Oyashio Current from the north, providing access to a wide variety of fresh fish.

Additionally, the livestock industry thrives, supported by a rich natural environment and abundant groundwater. Ibaraki Prefecture's original Wagyu beef, “Hitachi Beef,” has gained significant attention in recent years. Among its brands, the pinnacle “Hitachi Beef Kira” is distinguished by its commitment to the melting temperature of its fat and the fineness of its marbling—a hallmark of Wagyu—being the first in Japan to incorporate scientific indicators of deliciousness.

And the “Hitachi no Kagayaki” pork, a unique brand from Ibaraki Prefecture raised under strictly defined methods, won the gold prize in a competition for Japan's best pork for tonkatsu. Ibaraki Prefecture is also home to other appealing ingredients, such as the “Okukuji Shamo” chicken, which was crowned Japan's most delicious local chicken.
